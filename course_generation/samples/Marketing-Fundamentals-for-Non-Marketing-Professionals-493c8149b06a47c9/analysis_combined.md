Objectives:
## Learning Objectives

### Objective Overview
| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Primary Focus** | Cognitive (Knowledge & Application) |
| **Bloom's Level** | Evaluate |
| **Measurability** | 80 % of learners demonstrate mastery on each objective |
| **Time to Achieve** | 12 weeks |

### Primary Objectives
| Objective | Action Verb | Specific Outcome | Success Criteria |
|-----------|-------------|------------------|------------------|
| 1 | Analyze | Identify the four Ps of marketing and assess their applicability to a selected product/service case | Score ≥ 80 % on a scenario‑based quiz and provide a written justification |
| 2 | Evaluate | Critique three market‑research techniques and design a survey instrument for a target segment | Submit a survey prototype that meets rubric criteria and receives an instructor rating ≥ 4/5 |
| 3 | Create | Develop a 30‑minute digital‑marketing campaign plan using a chosen social‑media platform | Deliver a campaign brief, content calendar, and KPI dashboard that earns ≥ 4/5 on rubric |

### Secondary Objectives
| Objective | Skill Category | Transfer Value | Assessment Method |
|-----------|----------------|----------------|-------------------|
| 1 | Technical (Data literacy) | Ability to interpret basic market data in cross‑functional meetings | Data‑analysis lab with peer review |
| 2 | Technical (Social‑media content creation) | Crafting engaging posts for internal branding initiatives | Canva‑based content creation assignment |
| 3 | Soft skill (Communication) | Presenting marketing insights to non‑marketing stakeholders | 5‑minute presentation evaluated by rubric |

### Assessment Alignment
| Learning Objective | Assessment Type | Performance Indicator | Measurement Tool |
|--------------------|-----------------|----------------------|------------------|
| Primary Objective 1 | Summative | Accurate identification and justification of the 4Ps | Scenario‑based quiz (SCORM) |
| Primary Objective 2 | Summative | Quality of survey design and critical evaluation | Survey prototype submission & rubric |
| Primary Objective 3 | Summative | Completeness and feasibility of digital‑marketing plan | Campaign brief and KPI dashboard |
| Secondary Objective 1 | Formative | Correct interpretation of data visualizations | Interactive data‑lab |
| Secondary Objective 2 | Formative | Engaging social‑media content | Canva assignment submission |
| Secondary Objective 3 | Formative | Clarity and persuasiveness of presentation | 5‑minute video presentation rubric |

Audience:
## Audience Analysis

### Learner Demographics
| Attribute | Primary Profile | Secondary Profile |
|-----------|----------------|-------------------|
| **Job Roles** | Operations Manager, Finance Analyst, HR Lead, Product Manager | Sales Representative, Customer Service Manager, IT Specialist, Public Relations Coordinator |
| **Experience Level** | Mid‑level (3‑7 years in current role) | Senior‑level (8+ years) |
| **Industry** | Manufacturing, Finance, Healthcare, Technology, Retail | Public Sector, Non‑Profit Organizations |
| **Education** | Bachelor’s degree in Business, Finance, Engineering, HR, or related field | Minimum: High school diploma or equivalent; optional: Associate’s degree |

### Prior Knowledge Assessment
| Knowledge Area | Required Level | Assumption Basis | Gap Mitigation |
|----------------|----------------|------------------|----------------|
| Marketing Fundamentals | None | Target audience has no formal marketing education | Introductory module with glossary and real‑world analogies |
| Spreadsheet Proficiency | Basic | Most non‑marketing professionals use Excel/Sheets for reporting | 30‑minute refresher lab on filtering, pivot tables, and basic formulas |
| Data Analytics Tools | None | Limited exposure to analytics platforms | Hands‑on labs with Google Analytics 4 and Tableau Public |

### Learner Characteristics
| Characteristic | Primary Audience | Considerations | Design Implications |
|----------------|------------------|----------------|--------------------|
| **Motivation** | Contribute effectively to marketing initiatives; improve cross‑functional collaboration; enhance career prospects | Intrinsic: personal growth; Extrinsic: recognition, promotion | Embed real‑world case studies and measurable outcomes; provide certificates |
| **Time Constraints** | 3‑5 hours per week | Heavy workload, meetings, competing projects | Offer self‑paced micro‑learning modules (5‑10 min each); flexible deadlines |
| **Learning Style** | Visual (infographics, videos), Auditory (podcasts, narrated slides), Kinesthetic (hands‑on labs) | Preference for multimodal content | Integrate short videos, interactive dashboards, and simulation labs |
| **Technology Access** | Desktop/laptop, mobile device, broadband internet | Some may use older browsers | Ensure LMS is mobile‑responsive, supports HTML5 video, and meets WCAG 2.1 AA |

### Accessibility Requirements
| Need Category | Specific Requirements | Implementation | Success Metrics |
|---------------|----------------------|----------------|-----------------|
| **Visual** | Screen reader compatibility, large text options, high color contrast | WCAG 2.1 AA compliance, alt text for all images, scalable fonts | Compliance audit score ≥ 95% |
| **Auditory** | Captions for all videos, transcripts for podcasts | Provide SRT files and downloadable transcripts | 100% of video content captioned |
| **Motor** | Keyboard navigation, voice input support | ARIA landmarks, tab order testing | 100% of interactive elements keyboard‑accessible |
| **Cognitive** | Clear structure, chunked content, multiple formats | Storyboards, concise summaries, spaced repetition | Learner comprehension scores ≥ 80% on formative quizzes |
