## Presentation Plan

### Slide List
1. **Title:** Why Market Research Matters  
   - **Bullets:**  
     - ROI: data‑driven decisions boost profitability  
     - Risk reduction: spot market gaps before launch  
     - Opportunity identification: uncover unmet customer needs  
   - **Visual/Diagram:**  
     Icon‑based infographic showing ROI, risk, opportunity  
   - **Speaker Notes:**  
     Introduce the strategic value of research; give a quick case example of a brand that saved costs by using research.

2. **Title:** Techniques Overview  
   - **Bullets:**  
     - Primary methods: surveys, focus groups, interviews  
     - Secondary methods: industry reports, public datasets, social listening  
     - Process flow: Define → Collect → Analyze → Act  
   - **Visual/Diagram:**  
     Flowchart of the research cycle with icons for each method  
   - **Speaker Notes:**  
     Explain the difference between primary and secondary data; walk through the flowchart and highlight decision points.

3. **Title:** Designing an Effective Survey  
   - **Bullets:**  
     - Clear objectives aligned with research question  
     - Question types: Likert, multiple‑choice, open‑ended  
     - Pilot testing to refine wording and length  
     - Distribution plan: target segment, channels, incentives  
   - **Visual/Diagram:**  
     Diagram of survey design steps with arrows  
   - **Speaker Notes:**  
     Discuss best practices for question wording, avoiding bias, and the importance of a pilot test.

4. **Title:** AI & ML Trends in Market Research  
   - **Bullets:**  
     - Automated sentiment analysis in real time  
     - Predictive demand forecasting models  
     - Low‑code analytics democratizing data access  
   - **Visual/Diagram:**  
     Bar chart of AI adoption rates + AI icon  
   - **Speaker Notes:**  
     Highlight how AI tools can augment traditional research; give a brief example of a retailer using AI to adjust inventory.

5. **Title:** Common Misconceptions  
   - **Bullets:**  
     - “More data = better insights” – quality trumps quantity  
     - “Surveys are the only research method” – triangulation is key  
     - “AI can replace human judgment” – humans interpret context  
   - **Visual/Diagram:**  
     Myth vs. fact two‑column table  
   - **Speaker Notes:**  
     Use the slide to debunk myths and encourage critical thinking.

6. **Title:** Accessibility and Design Standards  
   - **Bullets:**  
     - Color contrast ≥ 4.5:1 for text, ≥ 3:1 for large text  
     - Alt text for all images and charts  
     - Captions for videos, keyboard navigation  
     - Use readable fonts (24‑pt headings, 18‑pt body)  
   - **Visual/Diagram:**  
     Checklist graphic with icons  
   - **Speaker Notes:**  
     Emphasize the importance of inclusive design; provide quick tips for presenters.

7. **Title:** Assessment Overview & Rubric  
   - **Bullets:**  
     - Formative labs: Survey prototype (20 min), Data analysis (20 min)  
     - Summative project: Digital Marketing Campaign Plan (40 %)  
     - Rubric criteria: Creativity, Feasibility, Metrics (≥ 80 %), Presentation Quality  
   - **Visual/Diagram:**  
     Table of rubric with weighted criteria  
   - **Speaker Notes:**  
     Walk through assessment expectations; explain how each criterion maps to learning outcomes.

### Visual Guidelines
- **Color/Contrast:**  
  Use a high‑contrast palette (dark blue text on light gray background) and ensure all charts meet WCAG 2.2 contrast ratios.  
- **Font/Size:**  
  Headings 28‑32 pt, body 18‑20 pt, sans‑serif (Arial or Calibri) for readability.  
- **Diagram Conventions:**  
  Use solid arrows for process flows, dashed for optional steps; label all axes and include units.

### Timing Plan
- **Total Slides:** 7  
- **Estimated Duration:** 35 minutes (≈ 5 min per slide + 10 min labs)  
- **Pace Notes:**  
  Pause after slide 3 for a quick Q&A; after slide 5 ask participants to identify a misconception; transition to labs after slide 6.
