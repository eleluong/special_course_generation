### Questions
1. **Type:** [MCQ]  
   - **Stem:** Which of the following best describes the role of *Price* in the marketing mix?  
   - **Options:**  
     - A) Determines the physical distribution channels.  
     - B) Influences the perceived value and revenue generation.  
     - C) Defines the product features and benefits.  
     - D) Sets the promotional messaging strategy.  
   - **Answer:** B  
   - **Rationale:** *Price* directly affects how customers perceive value and how much revenue the company can generate. Options A, C, and D relate to *Place*, *Product*, and *Promotion* respectively.  
   - **Alignment:** O1  

2. **Type:** [Short Answer]  
   - **Prompt:** In your own words, define *Market Segmentation* and explain why it is critical for a non‑marketing professional working in a cross‑functional team.  
   - **Expected Key Points:**  
     - Segmentation = dividing a broad market into subsets of consumers with common needs.  
     - Enables targeted product, pricing, place, and promotion decisions.  
     - Facilitates efficient resource allocation and clearer communication across departments.  
   - **Alignment:** O2  

3. **Type:** [Practical Task]  
   - **Task:** Using the eco‑friendly water bottle case study presented on Slide 3, create a 4Ps matrix that identifies:  
     1. Product features and positioning.  
     2. Pricing strategy and justification.  
     3. Distribution channels (Place).  
     4. Promotion mix and key messages.  
   - **Submission:** Upload a PDF of your matrix and a 2‑page narrative (≈ 400 words) explaining how each P supports the overall business objective.  
   - **Rubric:**  
     - **Criterion 1 – Completeness (40 %)** – All four Ps are fully described.  
     - **Criterion 2 – Alignment (30 %)** – Matrix aligns with the company’s value proposition.  
     - **Criterion 3 – Justification (20 %)** – Reasoning for each decision is evidence‑based.  
     - **Criterion 4 – Presentation (10 %)** – Clear, professional formatting and use of visuals.  
   - **Alignment:** O3
