## Presentation Plan

### Slide List
1. **Title:** What Is Marketing? – Definition, Evolution, and Strategic Role  
- **Bullets:**  
  - Customer‑centric function that creates value and drives business outcomes.  
  - Evolved from mass advertising to data‑driven, AI‑enabled personalization.  
  - Aligns product, sales, finance, and operations around a shared customer promise.  
  - Serves as the bridge between market insights and operational execution.  
  - Sets the foundation for the 4Ps framework.  
- **Visual/Diagram:**  
  - Timeline graphic showing marketing evolution: 1990s → Advertising, 2000s → Digital, 2010s → Data & AI, 2025 → AI‑driven personalization & hybrid touchpoints.  
- **Speaker Notes:**  
  - Define marketing as a customer‑centric, value‑creating function.  
  - Highlight the shift from traditional advertising to AI‑driven personalization.  
  - Emphasize cross‑functional alignment (product, sales, finance, operations).  
  - Mention that this evolution sets the stage for the 4Ps.  
  - Prompt a quick poll: “What does ‘marketing’ mean to you?” (30 s pause).

2. **Title:** The 4Ps of Marketing – Product, Price, Place, Promotion  
- **Bullets:**  
  - **Product:** features, benefits, differentiation, lifecycle.  
  - **Price:** perceived value, pricing models, psychological pricing.  
  - **Place:** distribution channels, omnichannel access, inventory.  
  - **Promotion:** messaging, media mix, digital tactics, storytelling.  
  - Each P influences the others; alignment is critical.  
- **Visual/Diagram:**  
  - 4Ps wheel with core vs. digital extensions (e.g., Digital Promotion, Subscription Pricing).  
- **Speaker Notes:**  
  - Explain each P with a brief real‑world example.  
  - Discuss digital extensions that modern marketers use.  
  - Address common misconception: “Marketing = Advertising.”  
  - Highlight AI’s role in pricing, place, and promotion.  
  - Transition to the case study with a 5‑minute interactive worksheet.

3. **Title:** Applying the 4Ps: Real‑World Example – Case Study Mapping & Gap Analysis  
- **Bullets:**  
  - Select a familiar product (e.g., eco‑friendly water bottle).  
  - Map current product features, pricing, distribution, promotion mix.  
  - Identify gaps: missing digital channels, pricing misalignment, promotion inconsistency.  
  - Propose actionable improvements: subscription model, influencer partnership, data‑driven promotion.  
  - Evaluate success metrics: sales lift, CAC, NPS.  
- **Visual/Diagram:**  
  - Case‑study flowchart: Current State → Gap Analysis → Recommended 4Ps Adjustments → Projected Outcomes.  
- **Speaker Notes:**  
  - Walk through the case study step‑by‑step.  
  - Engage learners in mapping and gap analysis.  
  - Tie proposed improvements to measurable KPIs.  
  - Conclude with a 10‑minute group discussion and a 5‑minute Q&A.

### Visual Guidelines
- **Color/Contrast:**  
  - High‑contrast dark text on light background.  
  - Use brand colors with WCAG 2.2 AA contrast ratio ≥ 4.5:1.  
- **Font/Size:**  
  - Title: 36 pt, Subtitle: 28 pt, Body: 24 pt, Bullets: 20 pt.  
  - Sans‑serif typeface (e.g., Arial, Helvetica).  
- **Diagram Conventions:**  
  - Icons for each P, color‑coded, ≤ 4 elements per diagram.  
  - Include descriptive alt‑text for all visuals.  
  - Use simple, clean layouts to aid quick comprehension.

### Timing Plan
- **Total Slides:** 3  
- **Estimated Duration:** 45 minutes  
  - Slide 1: 15 min (includes 30 s poll)  
  - Slide 2: 15 min (5 min worksheet, 10 min discussion)  
  - Slide 3: 15 min (10 min group work, 5 min Q&A)  
- **Pace Notes:**  
  - Pause after Slide 1 for poll results.  
  - Switch modality to interactive worksheet on Slide 2.  
  - Encourage live discussion and breakout groups on Slide 3.  
  - End with a brief recap and next‑step resources.
